Rafa Nadal has put together a great tennis center in his home island of Manacor. Good to Great Tennis Academy(DANDERYD. Sweden)


Good To Great is a Swedish Tennis Academy owned and run by former pro players Magnus Norman, Nicklas Kulti and Mikael Tillstrom. They call themselves a "Boutique Tennis Academy" where spots are limited. The academy has 6 different programmes available depending on the commitment of the junior. With their full time elite programme,players train 9 - 16 times a week, over 6 days. Performance group training will take place three to four per week and individual fitness programs are customised per student.

Kim Cijisters (Bree, Belgium)


The Kim Clijsters Academy like the Good To Great Academy refers to itself as a "boutique" tennis academy. Run by multiple grand slam winner Kim Clijsters and one of the best fitness trainers in the world Carl Meas, the KCA provides a nice friendly home for all levels of tennis players to hone their skills and progress their tennis level. I've heard a few people say how friendly the environment is there with the Clijsters family making you feel like you are at home.

Mouratoglou Tennis Academy (Biot, France)


Probably the most famous academy in the World,The Mouratoglou Academy has worked with best Junior players in the world to the best Pro players in the world. Founded in 1996 by Patrick Mouratoglou, the academy has grown to be an incredible tennis academy with impressive facilities, great coaches and a high level of players. Patrick and the Mouratoglou team has helped Serena Williams win countless Grand Slams and now with the up and coming Tsitsipas on the book, it wont be long before he gets his hands on a Slam with the help of Mouratoglou.

IMG Tennis Academy (Florida, USA)


Established nearly 40 years ago, the IMG Tennis Academy is the most well known Tennis Academy in the world. It has set the standard by which all academies are measured worldwide. They provide customised individual development plans and tournament schedules to set each athlete up for success to reach their peak potential. They've trained the best players in the world since Andre Agassi, Pete Sampras, Jim Courier, Tommy Haas, Anna Kournikova, Tommy Haas, Monica Seles and more recently Kei Nishikori, Maria Sharapova, Ryan Harrison and many more.
